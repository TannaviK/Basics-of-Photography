Apperture | ISO | Shutter Speed

The Apperture sets the amount of light that enters through the octagonal shaped blades of the lens.
Low Apperture values means a higher f-stop. Therefore, more light enters the lens, generating greater Exposure in the photograph.
Low Apperture also means more depth of field and the expectation of seeing Bokeh effects, and such.
Stay tuned for more updates on the various effects you can see in images, and photoshop tutorials!

ISO

The ISO is the amount of brightness in your end photo result, and is used to change Exposure very often.
High ISO causes a lot of unappreciated Noise in the photograph.
Hence, it is usually the last resort in low light photography.

Shutter Speed

The Speed of the shutter allows light to fall into the sensor for a controllable fraction of a second.
High shutter speed values are used for sports and action photography. It usually makes images have more clarity as well as more is in focus.
Image stablilisation in a lot of cameras has helped with this setting as high shutter speeds means less light enters.
